i.    Simplifying Algebraic Expression


Although there is much more to simplifying algeraic expressions, this discusion is ample for the next few chapters.  We are not, for now, going to allow powers on variables.  Thus, an algebraic expression may contain variable terms and constant terms.  Simplifying then is a matter of collecting together the costants to find one value and collecting variable terms together by their variable.

From this point forth, we will first eliminate subtraction and division from our equations when dealing with constants.  Notice that what we referred to as collecting like terms is actuallly using the distributive property in reverse.

However, this step is typically omitted in favor of simply adding the coeffients and calling it collecting like terms.  Mostly I bring up this point to quickly justify why we do this the way we do.

ii.    Evaluating Expressions When Variables’ Value is Known

The process I am about to discuss is one of the most important thus far as it will serve as our primary and most fundamental means of checking solutions throughout algebra.  Recall that evaluate means to follow through the order of operations until we arrive at a single numerical value.  It is an important concept to understandthat if two things are equal, one can be replaced by the other in an expression without changing the value of that expression.
