i.  Additive Identity and Additive Inverse

The Additive Identity then is a number that can be added to any other number without changing its value.  Throughout this book, we will always be referring to this number as zero, 0.  It is worthy of note that not all number sets have such a number though.  This will be discussed in the section on number groups.
